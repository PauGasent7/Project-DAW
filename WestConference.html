WEST CONFERENCE

The Western Conference is one of the two conferences, along with the Eastern Conference, that make up the organizational structure of the NBA. Founded in 1946, both are composed of fifteen teams and organized into three divisions of five teams each.

It was called the "Western Division" until 1970, when it was renamed the "Western Conference".

A total of 8 teams qualify for each conference. The leaders of each division mathematically qualify for the playoffs, while the remaining 5 teams will be those with the best win-loss record.
